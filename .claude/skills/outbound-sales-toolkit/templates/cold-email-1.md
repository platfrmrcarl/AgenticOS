# Initial Cold Email Template (Signal-Based)

Use this for the first touch in an outbound sequence. Personalize the **signal** for each recipient — the rest stays mostly the same.

---

## Template

```
Subject: noticed your [signal in 2-4 words]

Hey [first name] —

[1 line referencing the SPECIFIC signal you saw — what they did/posted/announced]

[1 line connecting that signal to a problem your product solves — be concrete, not vague]

[1 line low-commitment ask — usually a question, sometimes a 15-min call]

[Your first name]
[calendar link OR website link, not both]
```

---

## Worked example: targeting eng leaders at Series A startups

**Signal source**: LinkedIn post about hiring a Founding Engineer for AI work, last 14 days.

```
Subject: noticed your founding eng (AI) post

Hey Sarah —

Saw the Founding Engineer (AI) post you shared last Tuesday — looks like a great role.

Hot take: the painful part of that hire isn't finding the eng, it's defining what "AI" means at Acme without a senior AI hire to scope it. We've watched a few teams spend the search window with nothing shipping.

We help early teams ship their first agent in <2 weeks while the founding eng search runs. Worth 15 min on whether the timing problem resonates?

— Carl
[calendar.platfrmr.com/carl]
```

**Why this works:**

- ✅ Signal is specific and recent (LinkedIn post, last Tuesday)
- ✅ Hook isn't fake-flattery — names a real second-order problem
- ✅ Body shows you've thought about *their* situation, not pitched yours
- ✅ Ask is specific and low-commitment ("the timing problem")
- ✅ Length: 65 words

## Bad version (for contrast)

```
Subject: Quick question

Hi Sarah,

I noticed Acme is in the SaaS space and thought you'd be interested in our AI-powered platform that helps companies like yours drive efficiency and unlock growth through agentic automation.

We've helped many companies achieve great results. Are you open to a 15-minute call this week to learn more?

Best,
Carl
```

**Why this fails:**

- ❌ "Quick question" subject is meaningless
- ❌ "I noticed Acme is in the SaaS space" — not a signal, not specific
- ❌ "AI-powered platform that helps companies like yours" — pure marketing speak
- ❌ "Drive efficiency and unlock growth" — empty
- ❌ "We've helped many companies achieve great results" — unspecific = unbelievable
- ❌ Ask is about your needs ("learn more"), not theirs

## Variations by signal type

### Signal: They posted publicly about a pain point

```
Subject: re: your [topic] post

Hey [name] —

Your post about [specific pain] resonated — we hear that exact problem from [type of company] all the time.

[1 sentence about how your product addresses it, citing one specific way]

If we're at all relevant, I'd be happy to show you in 15 min. If not, no worries.

[Your name]
```

### Signal: They just raised a round

```
Subject: congrats on the [round]

[Name] —

Congrats on the [round] — saw the announcement on [day].

The reason I reached out: post-funding, [type of company] usually [common pain point]. We help by [specific solution in one line].

Open to a 15-min on whether this is on your roadmap?

[Your name]
```

### Signal: Competitor switch / job posting / tool stack change

```
Subject: noticed you're hiring for [role]

Hey [name] —

[Specific observation about the hire/change].

[Why this signal suggests they have the problem you solve, in one line.]

[Specific 1-line offer to help with that problem, with a low-commitment ask.]

[Your name]
```

---

## Pre-send checklist

- [ ] Subject is 4-6 words, lowercase, no emoji
- [ ] Signal is specific and **<14 days old**
- [ ] Body is **under 75 words**
- [ ] No mail-merge tokens left visible (`{firstName}`, etc.)
- [ ] One CTA only
- [ ] Sending from non-primary domain (e.g., `try.platfrmr.com`)
- [ ] Plain text, no tracking pixels, minimal links (calendar OR site, not both)
- [ ] You'd be willing to defend this email if the recipient called you out
